Tips and Info

Savings a little slow?

Why not help your mum wash the dishes for a few extra pennies?

Balance

The amount of money you have in your savings account.

Bank

A place where your savings can be kept safe.

Bank account

Where you can keep your money instead of carrying it all in your pockets!

Budget

To work out how much you should spend each day, week or month, so you can be sure you’re not spending more money than you have.

Debit Card

A card you can use in shops, online or over the phone to pay for things. It also lets you take money from your account at cash machines – like a key to your safe.

Cash Card

A card you use to take money from your account through going to cash machines – like a key to your safe.

Interest

The reward you get for saving money with a bank. It’s also something you pay when you borrow money from a bank.

Savings

The money you keep to one side for things you want in the future.

Savings Account

Like a money box or a coin jar. Just bigger and safer!

Earnings

Money that you get paid for work that you do.

Expenditure or spending

The amount of money you use to buy things.

Income

Any money you receive from working, or given to you by friends or family.

Deposit

Money that you put into a bank account.

Withdrawal

Money that you take out of a bank account.

Statement

Information your bank sends you by post or by email, showing what’s gone in and out of your bank account.

Getting Started.

  1. Hello! To start, slide the red circles to set your savings. You can also put in the money you make weekly, monthly and yearly. For more help, click the rectangular buttons.
  2. See how much you’ll save as you get older, by lifting the tape measure and growing your Mini Me. When your goal badge lights up, it means you’ve reached your goal.
  3. Click on the light bulb for top tips on money and saving.
